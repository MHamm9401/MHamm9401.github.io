I am a Biomedical Engineering student that is interested in both in-silico and in-vitro modeling of cardiovascular devices and anatomy. Outside of engineering, my hobbies include macro photography and rock-climbing.

Cardiovascular Flow Modeling Lab

I have worked under Dr. Vitaliy Rayz and Dr. Neal Patel in the Cardiovascular Flow Modeling Lab since my sophomore year. Under their outstanding mentorship, I have developed both a passion for computational modeling and the skills needed to be a successful researcher. My major projects were on morphological risk factors in cerebral aneurysms and machine learning for enhancing 4D flow MRI, and I have had the opportunity to present at multiple research conferences.

Cook Research Incorporated

I worked as an intern on Cook Research Inc.'s bench testing team for 17 months. My work had four main components:

  1. Performing verification & validation testing of endovascular devices
  2. Designing and validating state-of-the-art test methods
  3. Collaborating with Computer-Aided Engineering to align in-vitro and in-silico models
  4. Writing regulatory justifications in support of international regulatory submissions
I was able to have a broad range of projects that fostered a passion for in-vitro modeling and taught me to be adaptable and innovative.
